CityScope Tongji

What's CityScope ?

CityScope is a concept for shared, interactive computation for urban planning.
City Science researchers are developing a slew of tangible and digital platforms dedicated to solving spatial design and urban planning challenges. The tools range from simulations that quantify the impact of disruptive interventions in cities to communicable collaboration applications.[Source]

Milestone

In 2017, Tongji-MIT City Science Lab (CSL) was founded, the original version of CityScope(CityMatrix) is a tangible interface built with LEGO bricks.
In 2018, CityScope Shanghai LivingLine, as part of N-ICE 2035 project, modeled and conducted quantitive urban data research with machine learning upon the Ubi-data collected from residential neighborhood streets.
In 2019, Upon the framework of Cityscope and Living Line, an experimental project, led by Prof. Xiaohua Sun, was carried collaboratively out with the Center for Digital and Innovation (CDI) and CSL.
The ambition was to enlarge the Scope, from a static specific region to an elastic and dynamic way, like a whole City. Regarding that urban data, usually in spatial format, require multiple visualizations with various folded variables, augment reality is a decent solution with fancy interaction. As a result, a new version of CityScope was under prototyping.

Key Achievement

After defining the target user, usage scenarios with requirements by cases like urban planning, intelligent transportation, commercial activities analyzing, public security, and environment monitoring with Ubi-computing, demographic, etc. In order to rapidly sketch, transportation data was taken into prototyping with a region of Yangpu District.


Implemented Sketch

  • Mutiuser interaction Design, presneter <=> audience collaboration
  • Augmented reality & Touch Interaction
  • Spatial Extensibility - zoomable from a street or community to a district
  • Multiplatform(50inch desktop touchscreen, mobile screens, Radar info screen) data & visual synchronization.
  • Enrichment in the data layer, and spatial tiles
  • Sharing to Yangpu Government as a potential proposal for Smart City


Unfinished Conception

  • Advanced visual design
  • Lower time delay
  • Tangible sensor-based precision input device, like Surface Dial
  • Large dataset and realdate embedded
  • Automated data analyzing with Machine Learning
