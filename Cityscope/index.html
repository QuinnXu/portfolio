CityScope Tongji

What's CityScope ?

CityScope is a concept for shared, interactive computation for urban planning.
City Science researchers are developing a slew of tangible and digital platforms dedicated to solving spatial design and urban planning challenges. The tools range from simulations that quantify the impact of disruptive interventions in cities to communicable collaboration applications.[Source]

Milestone

In 2017, Tongji-MiT City Science Lab (CSL) was founded, the original vesrion of CityScope is an tangible interface built with LEGO bricks. [Learn More]
In 2019, CityScope Shanghai LivingLine, as part of N - ICE 2035 project,modelled and conducted a quantitive urban data research with machine learning upon the ubi-data collected froma residential neighborhood street. [Learn More]
In 2019 Summer, Upon the framework of Cityscope and Living LIne, an experimental project, led by Prof. Xiaohua Sun, was carried collabrately out with Center of Digital Innovation (CDI) and CSL. The ambition was to enlarge the Scope, from a static specifc region to an elastic and dyamic way, like a whole City. Regarding that urban data, usually in spatial format, require multiple visualisation with various folded variables, augment reality is a decent solution with fancy interation. As a , a new version of CityScope was under prototying.

Key Achievement

After defining the target user, usage scenrio with requirments by cases like urban planning, intelligent transportation, commercial activities analysing, public security and environment monitoring with ubi-computing, demographic and etc. , transportation data was took into prototyping with region of Yangpu District.
